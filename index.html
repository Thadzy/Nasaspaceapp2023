ABOUT THE EMIT

What is EMIT ?

A NASA instrument in orbit around the Earth and designed to study airborne particulate matter and its impact on global climate change has detected a large source of methane gas emissions. The EMIT device can pinpoint the location. Up to 50 large methane releases occur within a matter of months.

EMIT Detection

EMIT is designed to be able to determine the unique chemical composition of elements on Earth, and the fact that it can also detect methane is a happy coincidence. Scientists at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) explain that methane gas absorbs infrared radiation in a unique way that makes it detectable by EMIT devices. Which orbits the Earth with the International Space Station every 90 minutes at an altitude of approximately 420 kilometers, allowing the machine to scan various areas. You can go all over the world and go deep into some spots that are as small as a football field.
